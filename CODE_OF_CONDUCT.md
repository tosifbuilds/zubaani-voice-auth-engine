# 🛡️ The Zubaani Covenant: Code of Conduct

> **"Accessibility is not just about code; it is about human empathy. We cannot build inclusive technology without first building an inclusive community."**

## 🌟 Our Core Ethos

At Zubaani, we are engineering the future of digital accessibility through voice-first interactions. We believe that a community building tools for the "Next Billion Users" must fundamentally represent empathy, respect, and global inclusion. 

We pledge to make participation in the Zubaani ecosystem a secure, harassment-free, and highly collaborative experience for everyone—regardless of their technical background, identity, nationality, or experience level.

## 🤝 The Developer Standards (What We Value)

To maintain a world-class engineering culture, we expect every contributor to align with these core psychological principles:

* **Critique the Code, Not the Coder:** We give and receive feedback gracefully. Focus on architectural improvements, not personal flaws.
* **Lead with Empathy:** Remember that developers from different cultures and time zones are contributing. Assume positive intent in every Pull Request and Issue.
* **Elevate Others:** Share your knowledge. If a beginner opens a flawed Pull Request, guide them to the solution instead of shutting them down.
* **Radical Ownership:** If you make a mistake, own it, apologize, fix the code, and learn from the execution.

## 🚫 The Zero-Tolerance Zone

Zubaani operates on a strict zero-tolerance policy for toxic behavior. This ecosystem will immediately reject:

* **Trolling & Intellectual Arrogance:** Derogatory comments, elitism, or dismissing someone's valid questions.
* **Harassment of Any Kind:** Public or private harassment, sexualized language, or unsolicited advances.
* **Data & Privacy Violations:** Publishing others' private information (doxing) without explicit consent.
* **Disruptive Agendas:** Personal or political attacks that derail the community from our core mission of building accessibility tools.

## ⚖️ The Enforcement Protocol

As the core maintainers of Zubaani, we are deeply committed to protecting the mental bandwidth and psychological safety of our contributors. 

We hold the absolute right and responsibility to close Issues, reject Pull Requests, or permanently ban any contributor whose actions fundamentally violate this Covenant.
